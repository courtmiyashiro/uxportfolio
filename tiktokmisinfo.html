Countering Online Fake News and Misinformation on Short-Video Sharing Platforms

Project Overview


Throughout a full semester, I conducted user-centered research on the topic of fake news and misinformation on short-video sharing platforms.

The purpose of this study was to better understand user behaviors and judgement toward countermeasures for misinformation specifically on TikTok.

The study concluded with a full presentation report which highlighted the full literature review, screener survey, all interview notes/observations, audio/video recordings, and the Nvivo coding book.

Role


UX Researcher

Tools

Background

During my final semester of college, I took a course focusing on user-centered research and usability testing.

The course consisted of a semester-long project which would focus specifically on approaches to counter fake news and misinformation on short-video sharing platforms.

I underwent the full UX research process which consisted of determining goals, exploring research questions, choosing appropriate methods, and conducting interviews and testing.

Why is this important?

We are now living in an era where trust has been lost in institutions causing the increase of fake news on social media. Because of the increase in fake news and misinformation on popular platforms, tools and services are being advanced to help combat the spread.

Although there have been efforts to combat the spread of fake news and misinformation, there is still limited research on platforms such as TikTok and Instagram.

In order to find a solution to this problem, an understanding of users' behaviors, judgements, and attitudes toward misinformation countermeasures is essential. It is critical to gain insight into how users view misinformation content and why users promote it.

Research Goals


  • Understand the effectiveness of current approaches to counter fake news and misinformation on video-sharing platforms
  • Understand how to improve current approaches
  • Focus on platform affordances and the ad-hoc strategies they use

Research Questions


  1. How should approaches to counter fake news on short-video sharing platforms be designed to be accepted by the users of such platforms?
  2. How effective are the approaches in reducing the influence of fake news and misinformation?

Choosing Methods

Before conducting any user research, I had to determine my methods of collecting data for the study and establish a clear procedure.

1. Screener Survey

Finding ideal participants

2. Build Rapport

Understand media habits and familiarity with misinformation

3. Think-Aloud

Observe how selected participants interact with flagged TikTok Videos

4. Post-Interview

Understand participants' thoughts and opinions on counter approaches

Think-Aloud and Observations


Semi-structured interviews were conducted once I chose the ideal participants. The interview included a think-aloud and observation portion where the participant would interact with TikTok videos that had been identified as misinformation. This was done using a makeshift TikTok app that my professor had created on Expo Go.

Observations that were noted down included:

  • User flow of interacting with the videos
  • Features that were ignored
  • Attitudes toward the countermeasure and video itself
  • Facial expressions

Data Collection

Along with observations that were noted down, data was collected in several ways in order to analyze the participants' interview more closely.

I collected data in 4 ways including:

  • Audio recording using Voice Memos
  • Screen recording
  • Video recording
  • Audio transciption using Otter.ai

After gathering all forms of data, I used Nvivo and coded the transcriptions. By coding the audio transcriptions from my interviews, I was able to sort responses into specific themes.

Main Findings

After conducting interviews, I analyzed and transcribed all data that was collected. There were four main findings that stood out which included:

1. The placement of the misinformation countermeasure on the screen


2. The presentation and appearance of the misinformation countermeasure


3. The specificity of the information provided by the short-video sharing platform and fact-checker


4. The time at which the countermeasure was presented to the user throughout their user flow

Insights

Finding #1

"I like the no context warning at the bottom because it still allowed me to view the video and it didn't really interrupt."

Finding #2

"For COVID-19 related issues, there'll be a banner on the bottom that says "Get the facts COVID-19" and will take you to an actual website about the CDC, so I think they could do that for other big issues where there's misinformation, maybe they could add more information, link you to more credible information."

Finding #3

"If I'm going in with an open mind, it's helpful to read the comments to kind of see what other people are thinking and if they're thinking the same thing as I am, it's helpful to know that I'm not alone."

Finding #4

"I think with [the fact-checking feature] was fine, but it definitely gave me tunnel vision like I knew I was going into this was wrong so I wasn't even ready to listen."

Implications


The results suggested that users do pay attention to approaches that decrease the spread of fake news and misinformation. The results showed that a specific design approach can cause a user to question the credibility of a video; therefore, potentially limiting the video's intended effect to sway users.

As a result...


These results contribute to learning which countermeasures are most effective for users when using short-video sharing platforms. Future research and improvement to countermeasures would help combat fake news and misinformation which could prevent younger generations from being impacted.

Limitations

After drawing conclusions from the research findings, it was important to acknowledge limitations of the study which included:

  1. Limited time - only allowed for 2 interviews to be conducted
  2. Participants were mainly college students
  3. The study was limited to COVID-19 and did not extend to other topics where misinformation is significant

Takeaways

This semester-long study introduced me to what the full UX research process entails. As I progressed through each step of the process, I learned more of the do's and don'ts for collecting data and conducting surveys and interviews.

Key takeways from this study are:

  • User-centered research doesn't just include interviews - Before I took this specific course, I was not super familiar with the full process of conducting research. I generalized the process and knew it involved basic steps such as understanding the problem and conducting interviews. Because of this study, I learned that there is much more to the process such as finding current research on the problem and identifying the specific need for more research was important in order to progress.
  • Prepare for interviews thoroughly - I learned that the amount of preparation for conducting an interview had a direct effect on the success of the interview. Using an interview protocol, laying out all my questions, and having multiple ways of recording the interview helped tremendously. I was also able to become more comfortable with being the interviewer.
  • User-centered research is fun with the right topic - Before undergoing this study, I was intimidated with how much time it was going to take and all the steps. However, even through the times I got a bit stressed, I found the research process worthwhile because the study was on a topic that was still current and something I could relate to.

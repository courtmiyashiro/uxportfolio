Ole School Alumni Scholarship Group Capstone Project

Project Overview


A senior capstone project which was focused on throughout an entire semester. The project involved working with The Ole School Alumni Scholarship Group, a JMU alumni group who aims to connect students with mentors and internship, job, and scholarship opportunities.

The goal of this project was to apply all skills learned throughout the major and collaborate with CIS majors to develop a complete web system for the client.

My team placed within the Top 3 after Sprint #3 and overall Top 5 out of 12 teams after the final sprint.

Role


Front-End Developer and UX Designer

User Research, Front-End Development, Wireframes, Mockups, Material Gathering


Tools


Trello, Miro, Adobe XD, Visual Studio Code, Github, SQL, Canva

Background

I worked on an 11 person team consisting of nine Computer Information Systems majors and two Interactive Design majors including myself. Together, we were tasked with creating a system the Ole School Alumni Scholarship Group could use as an extension to their website. This system would be utilized by all members of the group to manage, edit, find scholarships, job, and internship opportunities as well as communicate.

Throughout the duration of the semester, my team and I attended several meetings with the client to understand their needs and current pain points.

The project was divided into 4 sprints, each sprint concluding with a presentation to the client and professors. Within each sprint, my team and I worked together to plan, design, and develop the system which would best serve the client.

As a member of the team I...
  • Established the design process
  • Worked closely with back-end developers
  • Lead the front-end development and made edits
  • Gathered and helped to finalize all final deliverable materials

Understanding the Problem

My team and I prioritized meeting with the client as soon as possible to collect critical information for Sprint 1. We assessed the current scenario and were able to gather the main pain points. These pain points included:

  • Organizational information is disorganized
  • Data gets lost due to unseen emails and clutter
  • Communication channels are not efficient

Establishing Project Goals

Once pain points were identified, we established the most important goals for the project and user needs.

The primary user needs for the system were develop a centralized system and database where all OSASG information can be edited, viewed, stored and have an easier way to communicate and store organizational data

Project goals included:

  • Improve user communication by developing a messaging system
  • Give mentors the ability to send opportunity and announcement updates to students
  • Have a database to track all mentor/student assignments
  • Give users the ability to view, edit, and update their own profiles

Research

In order to get a better insight into what users wanted, we survey students who were already apart of the Ole School Alumni Scholarship Group. Within this survey, we asked what they would like to see in the system and to offer up any websites they liked the design and functionality of. Using the results from the students, we were able to develop user stories and user personas for the primary target users.

Brainstorming and Wireframing

Once research was conducted and information was collected from client meetings, I brainstormed ideas for the design of the system and created low-fidelity wireframes outlining the potential look of it for Sprint 2.

Wireframes were created using Miro.

Inspiration


Once the low-fidelity wireframes were created, my team and I discussed what color schemes and other sites we could go off of for inspiration.

The client's main website used James Madison University colors, so we decided to stick with those core colors.

Along with the design we went with for the wireframes, I took the suggestions given by students from the survey as well as sites my team and I were familiar with into consideration. These sites included LinkedIn, Handshake, and SEO.

Prototype


My team and I showed the client the low-fidelity wireframes and made adjustments according to their feedback. After noting down additional needs from the client, I worked to create mid-fidelity wireframes. Once the proposed design was clear, I focused on the front-end design of the system for my team's system walkthrough for Sprint 3.

View Sprint 3 Walkthrough

Final Design

After submitting the walkthrough, my team and I continued to work to improve our system before our presentation to the client. I made adjustments based off of additional client feedback and worked to finalize the front-end development of the system. I worked closely with the back-end developers on my team, making sure the connection between front-end and back-end code was working. I maintained constant communication with the back-end developers ensuring that the proposed design in the prototype was going to be carried out.

The final system was hosted on AWS - System was only up throughout the remainder of the capstone

Results and Takeaways

Throughout this project, I was able to get a feel for what it is like to work on a team consisting of members who have different skills and backgrounds. This project was eye-opening and allowed me to experience real-world scenarios that would come up in a real job. I was able to put all of my knowledge into this project and work toward a goal by communicating and taking the lead in multiple areas.

Key takeways from this project are:

  • Listen to the client - Throughout this project, I learned from professors and experience with the client, that we have to listen to what the client wants. Regardless, if we disagree with their ideas or wants, we have to listen to them. However, we can still suggest our own design ideas to the client that could be beneficial.
  • Communication and planning is key - Earlier in the project, poor communication and planning made things even harder. I learned that if no one else was stepping up, then I had to be the one to push the team to communicate what progress was being made and initiate planning for team meetings.
  • Set aside differences - This was the very first time I had worked on a team with 10 other individuals. I learned that everyone has different opinions and different personalities. Sometimes these differences created a hostile team environment; however, I learned that I need to respect the individual no matter what. We are all working toward the same goal and need to value each other.

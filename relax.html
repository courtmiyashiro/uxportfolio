RELAX Meditation App

Project Overview


RELAX is a meditation app that helps users better their mental health. It emphasizes the importance of mental health in our current world, sending a message that you are not alone. RELAX is a free, effective, and more personal app that allows users to express, track, and cope with their mental health that can be caused by various factors of life.

The purpose of this project was to create an app that would be beneficial for every day mental health.

I took the lead role on a team of three in researching and designing RELAX.

Background

As an individual and student who has anxiety, mental health is a crucial part of my life that I am constantly trying to improve. Mental health tends to be seen as a touchy subject that isn't often focused on. We wanted to create an app that embraces mental health and assists individuals of all types in bettering their mental health.

It is recognized that the market for mental health awareness apps has begun to increase over the recent years; however, there have been evident user problems with these apps. With our user research, we strived to pinpoint issues with current mental health apps in order to make RELAX the most effective and user-friendly as possible.

Our goal was to create a calm, pleasant experience for the user and make it a positive resource to turn to.

Understanding the Problem

My group and I heavily discussed the overall problem that we were aiming to solve with this app.

As students, we felt that many people such as college students such as ourselves, adults, young and old, experience difficulties with handling and coping with their mentla health. The constant workload of classes, extra-curriculars, and jobs while trying to balance a social life is not easy. We acknowledged that all of this can result in stress, anxiety, and/or depression building up quickly, and many can find themeselves lost and frustrated with how to cope with it.

Our Problem Statement:

College students and adults need to be able to have a free, effective and more personal way to express, track, and cope with their mental health problems that can be caused by school, work, and personal life issues because it is important for those who struggle with their mental health to be able to have positive guidance without dealing with the stress of finding and affording good therapy or using ineffective subscription based apps that don't offer enough help.

The Product Vision

We identified the most important tasks that the app would deliver.

  • This app should require users to create an account with a valid email account for free
  • This app should allow users to take a survey/quiz which asks them questions about what they are feeling or experiencing and rank their stress/sadness/anxiety levels
  • This app should allow users to explore freely and listen/read/utilize all of the available coping mechanisms, helpful information, and sleep aids for free without a pricey subscription

Research

We began our research by reaching out to those who were seeking support, had previous experience with technology, and those who were willing to work with us to create an appropriate resource.

Our target users ranged from college students to working adults in their mid to late twenties.

We conducted interviews through phone calls and video chat. Interviewees' answers were recorded by screen recording, video recording, and written notes.

Information was shared and collected using Miro to allow for comparison. We created a Miro board which consisted of participant information and answers onto stick notes which corresponded to each interviewee.

User Personas

Through our research and participant responses from the interviews, we were able to get a clearer vision of who the target users for RELAX would be. We created a primary and secondary persona which would help us focus on the goals that the app would need to meet. Lucy was our primary persona and Kenneth was our secondary persona.

Design and Prototype

Before we started designing the prototype for RELAX, I constructed a site map which would layout the most essential elements and tasks that the app would include.

My team and I worked to design the low-fidelity prototype of the app using Adobe XD. This level prototype showed the basic layout and tasks I illustrated in the site map.

Final Design

My team and I took the feedback from our low-fidelity prototype and made necessary changes as well as began implementing color and images to create a high-fidelity prototype.

Feedback included suggestions regarding:

  • Consistent button style
  • Adding back or next buttons
  • Consideration of using a card style design

The final prototype for our RELAX app included:

  • A complete logo
  • Redesigned pages with a color scheme and images
  • Updated navigation bar
  • Full interaction with added wires for nearly every component

Takeaways

This project allowed me to become familiar with using UX/collaboration platforms such as Miro and Adobe XD. I was able to go through the full process of forming a design plan, conducting research, and carrying out the development of a complete app. Because of this project, I was pushed to take a lead and learn on the go.

Key takeaways from this project are:

  • Learning on the go is beneficial - Before this project, I had no experience using Adobe XD. With time and being hands-on with it, I was able to learn quickly. I was able to familiarize myself with how to use Adobe XD each and every time I worked to design the app. With limited time to create this app and an extensive amount of time using Adobe XD, learning on the go was very beneficial.
  • Communication is critical - Even though I was only working with two other individuals, communication was still difficult at times. Sometimes we are not all on the same page, and at times, it takes a person to clearly communicate and take the leadership role. I learned to become comfortable speaking up about what tasks needed to be completed and what needed to be fixed.
  • Creating an app requires a lot of time and analyzing - During this project, I found myself creating and designing a majority of the app. I learned that I had to carefully analyze every component especially when wiring components to each other. I realized that I had to have an eye for watching out for errors or else the app would not be correct.

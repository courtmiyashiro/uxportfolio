Here are some things you should know about me.

Why UX design?

When I arrived at JMU, I was actually a Computer Science major. I went through a semester and a half learning the basics of Python and realized that it just wasn't for me. I've always had an eye for appealing designs, colors, and aesthetics. I wanted to embrace that, so I made a leap and chose the Interactive Design concentration. The concentration opened the doors to UX and UI while also allowing me to learn how to code but still use my creativity.

Why minor in Computer Info Systems?

Being in the Interactive Design concentration, I focused on front-end development and thought it would benefit me to learn more about the back-end. I chose CIS as a minor because I could expand on my technical, communication, and team-building skills. It was a win-win, and I could broaden my knowledge in multiple areas such as system design, information architecture, and project management.
